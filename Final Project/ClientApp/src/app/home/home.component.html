History: A Timeline


1864

President Abraham Lincoln sets aside Yosemite Valley and the Mariposa Grove of Giant Sequoias. This was the first time the federal government protected land because of its natural beauty.

1872

Yellowstone National Park is established as the first National Park.

1906

President Theodore Roosevelt establishes Mesa Verde National Park, the first time a national park was created to conserve man-made objects and American history.

1916

President Woodrow Wilson signs the 'Organic Law' to create a National Park Service. This bureau was tasked to protect the 35 existing national parks, as well as future ones.

1933

The management of over fifty national monuments and military sites -- including Gettysburg National Military Park -- are transferred from the War and Agriculture Departments to the National Park Service. In the same year, President Franklin D. Roosevelt's New Deal program established the Cilvilian Conservation Corps, recruiting the unemployed to plant billions of trees, fight wildfires and build roads and trails.

2016

A century after the National Park Service was created, it is now responsible for more than 400 park locations in all 50 states, the District of Columbia, American Samoa, Guam, Puerto Rico, Saipan and the Virgin Islands. One of the newest additions is Stonewall National Monument -- the first national monument that honors the history of the Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual and Transgender community.


The National Mall is a landscaped park within the National Mall and Memorial Parks. Added to the National Register of Historic Places in 1966, it is an official unit of the United States National Park System.


Over 84 million acres for conservation and recreation!

The National Park Service preserves unimpaired the natural and cultural resources and values of the National Park System for the enjoyment, education, and inspiration of this and future generations. The Park Service cooperates with partners to extend the benefits of natural and cultural resource conservation and outdoor recreation throughout this country and the world.
